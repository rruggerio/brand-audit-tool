01

Project
Overview

Barnes & Noble needed to reimagine their digital presence and in-store experience to compete in an increasingly digital retail landscape. As the overall creative and UX lead, I directed a comprehensive transformation spanning web, mobile, and physical retail touchpoints.

The engagement encompassed brand content audits, customer archetype research, competitive analysis, and the development of an omni-channel strategy that successfully drove incremental growth during the critical 2015 holiday season.

Beyond digital, I led the creative direction for in-store digital activations, including a groundbreaking motion design initiative that brought book covers to life on in-store video displays—creating moments of discovery and delight for customers in the redesigned retail spaces.

02

The
Challenge

Competing in a Digital-First World

Barnes & Noble faced mounting pressure from Amazon and other digital-first retailers. Their digital experience was fragmented, their in-store and online channels operated in silos, and they were losing market share to competitors who had mastered omni-channel retail.

Digital Disconnect

Online and in-store experiences felt like separate companies, confusing customers and missing cross-channel opportunities.

Dated Experience

The digital ecosystem hadn't evolved with customer expectations, resulting in declining engagement and conversion rates.

Physical Retail Evolution

New store concepts with restaurants and bars needed digital experiences that matched the elevated physical environment.

Holiday Pressure

The 2015 holiday season was critical—they needed measurable improvements in digital channel performance quickly.

03

Our
Approach

We took a comprehensive, research-driven approach that balanced immediate needs with long-term transformation goals.

1. Research & Discovery

Conducted comprehensive brand content audits, customer archetype research, and competitive analysis to understand the landscape and identify opportunities.

  • → Customer journey mapping across digital and physical touchpoints
  • → Competitive benchmarking against Amazon, independent bookstores, and other retailers
  • → Content audit of existing digital properties and brand materials

2. Omni-Channel Strategy

Developed a unified strategy connecting online and in-store experiences, with specific focus on the critical holiday shopping period.

  • → Cross-channel customer experience design
  • → Holiday campaign strategy and execution
  • → Digital-to-physical and physical-to-digital conversion paths

3. Digital Ecosystem Redesign

Led the redesign of the corporate website and digital touchpoints with focus on improved navigation, content discovery, and conversion optimization.

4. In-Store Digital Innovation

Created motion design content for in-store video displays, transforming book covers into dynamic visual stories that enhanced the new retail concept.

04

Motion Design
Innovation

Bringing Books to Life

As Barnes & Noble redesigned its physical retail concept—adding reading areas, a full-service restaurant, and a bar—I led a team of three animators to create motion-design book art for wall monitors throughout the stores.

We leveraged book cover art to tell dynamic visual stories, creating moments of discovery and adding a signature animated element to the new store design. The motion graphics celebrated the joy of reading while modernizing the in-store experience.

The omni-channel strategy and digital improvements drove measurable results during the critical 2015 holiday season, while the in-store innovations enhanced the customer experience in redesigned retail locations.
