About Me

Who I Am &
How I Arrived Here

A 25+ year journey from curious child dismantling appliances to IBM Design Principal, driven by one question: why?

I am Richard Ruggerio, Design Principal & Executive Creative Director with IBM Consulting (IBM iX). Over my 25+ year career I have worked across a remarkable range of industries and brands—always driven by the same restless question my grandfather taught me to ask: why?

Growing up, my grandmother was a poet and my grandfather a furniture maker and painter. Their curiosity was infectious. My grandfather handed me old appliances and TVs to dismantle, then lego blocks to recombine—instilling in me a design philosophy I still carry: always dig deeper, tear things down, rebuild them to form a meaningful connection to others.

I joined IBM through an acquisition and have since become a member of the iX Global Creative Council, co-lead of the Design System Guild, and a named IBM Design Principal. I am proudest of the people I have had the privilege to lead and mentor—the strategists, UX/UI designers, researchers, and developers who make the work worth doing.

When I am not designing, I am making art or building things in my backyard studio. That creative practice shapes everything.

Design
Philosophy

Always Ask Why

My grandfather taught me to question everything. This curiosity drives me to dig deeper into problems, understand root causes, and create solutions that truly matter. Design isn't just about making things look good—it's about understanding why they exist and who they serve.

Tear Down, Rebuild, Connect

Like dismantling old appliances as a child, I believe in deconstructing complex systems to understand their components, then rebuilding them in ways that create meaningful connections between people and technology. Every project is an opportunity to reimagine what's possible.

People First, Always

The work is only as good as the people behind it. I'm proudest of the teams I've built and the designers I've mentored. Great design comes from diverse perspectives, collaborative thinking, and a shared commitment to excellence. Leadership means empowering others to do their best work.
