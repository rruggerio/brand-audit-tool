01

Project
Overview

Hertz Corporation needed to modernize their digital presence across three distinct rental car brands: Hertz, Dollar, and Thrifty. The challenge was to create a unified technical foundation while maintaining each brand's unique identity and customer experience.

As the design lead, I directed a cross-organizational team through the complete redesign of Hertz.com on Adobe AEM, establishing it as the shared experience platform and code base for all three brands. This involved creating an initial design system, migrating the team from Sketch & InVision to Adobe XD, and defining new collaborative processes.

The rental flow MVP launched successfully, and the project was recognized with the 2018 IBM CIA Project of the Year award for innovation and delivery excellence.

02

The
Challenge

Three Brands, One Platform

Hertz Corporation operated three distinct rental car brands, each with its own website, codebase, and customer base. Maintaining three separate digital properties was costly and inefficient, but each brand needed to maintain its unique market position and customer experience.

Technical Debt

Three separate codebases with outdated technology stacks, making updates slow and expensive across all brands.

Brand Differentiation

Each brand served different customer segments and needed to maintain distinct visual identities and experiences.

Tool Migration

Team was using Sketch & InVision. Needed to migrate to Adobe XD while maintaining productivity during the transition.

Cross-Org Collaboration

Multiple teams across different organizations needed to work together seamlessly on a shared platform.

03

Our
Approach

We took a systematic approach that balanced immediate business needs with long-term platform sustainability. The strategy centered on creating a flexible design system that could serve multiple brands while establishing efficient collaborative processes.

1. Design System Foundation

Established an initial design system that could flex to accommodate three distinct brand identities while sharing core components and patterns. This reduced redundancy while preserving brand differentiation.

  • → Created shared component library with brand-specific theming
  • → Defined design tokens for colors, typography, spacing, and other variables
  • → Documented patterns and usage guidelines for consistent implementation

2. Adobe AEM Implementation

Led the redesign of Hertz.com on Adobe AEM, establishing it as the shared technical foundation for all three brands. Worked closely with development teams to ensure design system components translated effectively to AEM.

  • → Designed component architecture aligned with AEM capabilities
  • → Created templates and page structures for content authors
  • → Established design-development handoff processes

3. Tool Migration & Team Enablement

Migrated the design team from Sketch & InVision to Adobe XD, providing training and establishing new workflows. This improved collaboration and aligned with Adobe's ecosystem (AEM, XD, Creative Cloud).

  • → Conducted Adobe XD training sessions for design team
  • → Established new file organization and naming conventions
  • → Created shared component libraries in Adobe XD

4. Rental Flow MVP

Prioritized the core rental flow as the MVP, focusing on the most critical customer journey. This allowed us to launch value quickly while continuing to build out the full platform.
